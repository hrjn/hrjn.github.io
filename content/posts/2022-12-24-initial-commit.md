This is not my first attempt at blogging. In fact I can't recall how many times I have tried being serious about it, the many domain names I bought without ever using them, the `blog`, `new_blog` and `new_new_blog` directories I scrapped from my computers with barely anything in them.

Today I spent quite some time cleaning up my RSS feed manager (I am moving away from [Feedly](https://feedly.com/) to [NetNewsWire](https://netnewswire.com/)) and many of the articles I saw were talking about the current mess at Twitter. I also have a Twitter account that I almost never used to tweet, but instead I got so much invaluable insights from the people I followed there: links to cool blog posts, lifesaver code snippets, nerdy jokes and memes... Unfortunately all of that may soon come to an end, and many of the people I follow have (or very soon will) stopped posting. 

However for a few of them I noticed something interesting. They announced that they would *get back to blogging*. No more length limit, no more nonsense blue label to feel important, just a good old static website and its RSS feed. Because at the end of the day, when you like sharing knowledge, you don't want to stop because of a platform shutdown, and you do what it takes to write and publish about topics you are passionate about. 

That is what inspired me for trying blogging once again. This is tentative N+1. And if you find yourself reading this initial post, it means that at least the first step was done. Some say that it is the most difficult one, so hopefully you will find content that you will enjoy down the road :)
